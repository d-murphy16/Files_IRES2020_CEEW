1. Introduction¶

  • Electricity is one of the most important blessings that science has given to mankind. It has also become a part of modern life and one cannot think of a world without it.

  • From household chores to trade & industrial growth, it plays a major role in our lives.

  • It is evident from that fact that World Bank mentions Getting Electricity as one of the prime indicators of Ease of doing Business.

  • Primary conventional energy sources for generating electricity are Coal, Lignite, Gas, Diesel, Nuclear & Hydro while non-conventional renewable energy sources are Solar, Wind, Biogas etc.

  • Electricity is produced at generating plants run by companies called GENCOS, then is transmitted by effective transimmsion network run by companies called TRANSCOS & finally it is distributed to the residential/commercial premises by the DISCOMS

  • Though access to the electricity has increased upto a large extent in last 20 years (India being the energy surplus country), its reliability, effective distribution & satisfaction to the end consumer has remain the challenge.

2. Problem Statement¶

  • We all are dependable on electricity as a basic need. From basic household chores to leisiure at home with AC in the scrothing heat outside & safety in the steets at night, we require electricity.

  • Even though almost of 99% of the villages/towns/cities are electrified in India, access to the reliable electricity is the greatest challenge.

  • Many of the times, it happens that there are either prolonged power cuts or low volatge availability/flactutations.

  • Most of these problems happen in semi-urban/rural parts of the country. Hence, demographic study is the key here.

  • Factors like poverty, education/awareness, local distributor, policy support by the Governement etc. plays a major role.

  • Since renewable energy sources is assumed to be the future of distributed generation, awareness about these technolgies is also very important.

Scenario

  • **CEEW**, The Council on Energy, Environment and Water is one of Asias leading not-for-profit policy research institutions. CEEW uses data, integrated analysis, and strategic outreach to explain and change the use, reuse, and misuse of resources.

  • They conducted Indian Residential Energy Survey (IRES), 2020 which is a pan-India survey on energy access and consumption patterns in Indian households.

  • The survey covers:

  • IRES provides detailed information (a questionaaire containing more than 500 features) about the state of electricity and cooking energy access, energy consumption and expenditure patterns, equipment characteristics for major end-uses (cooking, lighting, space cooling, heating, entertainment, etc.), appliance purchase behaviour, and awareness about star labels and energy conservation behaviour among Indian households.

  • Checkout **Talks of CEEW** to get better clarity about the Survey.

Selection of Dataset

  • The above mentioned survey covers a range of topics from demography to state of electricity, energy consumption & expenditutre, appliance characteristics & purchase behaviour & awareness of coservation of energy.

  • As far as study & analysis performed in this sheet, *a subset of the raw data* has been selected. Rationale behind the selection has been presented below (Data acquisition & Description).
